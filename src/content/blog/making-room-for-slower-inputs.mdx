I do not want a personal homepage to behave like a productivity tool. It should not compete for attention, and it should not resemble an interface whose main goal is to increase throughput.

The better model is a desk:

- a few things visible at once
- obvious places to return to
- enough empty space that thought can settle

## What belongs on the page

For me, the durable pieces are writing and listening. Blog posts hold longer-form thinking. Album notes catch a different kind of memory: what a record felt like before it hardened into opinion.

That combination is useful because it makes the homepage less like a résumé and more like a stable surface for ongoing attention.

## Keep the structure honest

When a site only has a few sections, each one has to justify itself.

I would rather have:

- two categories that stay active
- short archives that remain browsable
- detail pages that are pleasant to reread

than ten placeholders for future ambitions.

The result is smaller, but it is also truer.
