Some albums sound bigger every year. *Helplessness Blues* does that for me, partly because its questions stay unresolved.

The writing keeps circling identity, usefulness, and the distance between private desire and public life. That could have made the record feel cramped. Instead, the harmonies and the room in the arrangements make it feel almost geographic.

It is an album I reach for when I need something thoughtful without becoming inert.
